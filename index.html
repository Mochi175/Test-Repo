Eat like a plant.

A whole-food, plant-predominant diet — built around vegetables, fruits, legumes, whole grains, nuts, and seeds — sits at the center of Kaiser Permanente's approach to preventing, and often reversing, chronic disease.

Diet-related disease is one of the leading causes of preventable death in the U.S. — most of it tied to the heart.

Tips to try

Start small, add as you go.

You don't have to change everything at once. Try adding one of these habits at a time, and build from there.

Add a piece of fruit to every meal — berries are an easy place to start.

Fill half your plate with vegetables at lunch and dinner, including leafy greens.

Work beans, lentils, or peas into 3–4 meals during the week.

Swap in a whole grain — brown rice, quinoa, or oats — at every meal.

Accent meals with a small serving of avocado, nuts, or seeds for healthy fat.

Season generously with fresh or dried herbs and spices instead of salt.

Eating on a budget

Plant-based doesn't mean pricey.

A little planning goes a long way toward making healthy eating affordable — here's where to focus.

Plan ahead

  • Set aside time each week to plan meals and write a shopping list.
  • Double a recipe and freeze half for a night you don't feel like cooking.

Shop smart

  • Buy produce in season, or reach for frozen vegetables — just as nutritious, and cheaper.
  • Check the bulk bins for beans, rice, oats, and other staples.
  • Compare store-brand options with name brands.

Cook smart

  • Make vegetables the main dish and treat meat as a smaller side.
  • Use beans or lentils instead of meat a few nights a week.
  • Batch-cook a big pot and freeze portions for later.
